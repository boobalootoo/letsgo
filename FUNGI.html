Fungi

Mushrooms and Toadstools (Cap-and-Stem Fungi)
Puffballs and Earthstars
These are round fungi that release spores when they mature.
Coral Fungi and Club Fungi
These fungi have branch-like or club-shaped structures.
Lichens (Symbiotic Fungi)
Lichens are a combination of fungi and algae that usually grow on trees, rocks, or other surfaces.
Polypores (Bracket Fungi)
These fungi grow in layers or shelves on trees, logs, or stumps.
Rusts, Smuts, and Other Parasites
These fungi often affect plants and may appear as spots or streaks on leaves.
Jelly Fungi and Molds
These fungi are typically gelatinous or slimy in texture and may look like gels or blobs.
Crust Fungi and Skin-like Fungi
These fungi have flat or crusty bodies that adhere to surfaces, such as tree bark or dead wood.
